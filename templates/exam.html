Privacy & proctoring notice

Before you start, read this notice carefully. By continuing, you confirm that you understand how ExamGuard AI processes information during this session. This is a summary for transparency; your school or employer may have additional policies.

Camera & video

Your browser may access your webcam so the system can analyze live frames on the server (or as configured) for proctoring signals such as face count, approximate head pose, lighting, and similar cues. Video is not necessarily recorded in full; snapshots or frames may be saved when a potential integrity issue is flagged.

Automated analysis (AI)

Computer-vision checks run on submitted frames. Results are probabilistic and can be wrong. Flagged events are logged for review by authorized staff—not automatic disciplinary decisions.

Exam answers & question types

This assessment may include multiple-choice (MCQ), short answer, and written items. All responses you submit are stored with your registration details and timestamp. MCQs may be auto-scored; short and written responses are typically reviewed by your instructor.

Behaviour & device signals

  • Events such as leaving the exam tab, window focus changes, blocked shortcuts, and similar browser-level signals may be logged as security events.
  • Technical metadata (e.g. timestamps, session identifiers) may be stored for audit purposes.

Who can see your data

Authorized administrators and instructors for this deployment can view violations, evidence images, submitted answers, and related logs. Email alerts may be sent to configured administrator addresses when serious flags occur.

Retention & location

Data is stored on servers or devices running this application (e.g. local database and violations/ folder for images). Retention periods depend on your institution—ask them for deletion or export if applicable.

Your responsibilities

Do not impersonate others. Do not share exam content without permission. Follow all instructions from your invigilator or institution.

If you do not agree with camera access, automated analysis, and logging described above, do not proceed—contact your instructor or exam coordinator.
